Outro to F1

Now that you know a bit about the drivers and why I love this sport, here's everything you need to actually start watching and following along.

Where to Watch & Learn

There are plenty of ways to watch F1 depending on your budget. You can pay for it through ESPN, wait for it to air on TV, or — full transparency — do what I do and find a stream online. I won't get into specifics, but if you look it up you'll find it pretty easily.

If you want to learn more beyond this blog, some great starting points are the ESPN F1 page, YouTube guides, and F1 content creators on social media. There's genuinely something for every type of fan out there, whether you want deep technical breakdowns or just fun highlights.

Terms You Should Know

DRS (Drag Reduction System) — A movable flap on the rear wing of the car that opens to reduce drag and boost straight-line speed. Drivers can only activate it in designated zones when they're within one second of the car ahead, making it one of the main overtaking tools in the sport.

Undercut & Overcut — Both are strategic pit stop moves. The undercut means pitting earlier than your rival to get fresher tyres and gain track position. The overcut means staying out longer while your rival pits, banking time while they're sitting in the pit lane.

Safety Car — Deployed when there's an incident on track that needs marshals or debris clearance. The whole field bunches up behind it, which can completely shake up the race order.

Virtual Safety Car (VSC) — A slower, electronic version of the safety car period where all drivers must reduce their speed by a set percentage. Used for less serious incidents that don't require a full safety car.
