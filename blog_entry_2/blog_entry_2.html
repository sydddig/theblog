Me and F1

To get more personal — let's talk about my favorite drivers and my journey with F1.

Carlos Sainz

First up, and probably obvious: Carlos Sainz. Born September 1st, 1994, he grew up around motorsport — his dad was a Rally racing driver, and he has a mom and a few sisters. Over his career he's moved between several teams, with his most notable stint being at Ferrari alongside Charles Leclerc. He's now at Williams alongside Alexander Albon.

Carlos is actually the reason I got into F1, funnily enough. My friends and I went to see Gran Turismo in theaters, and one friend got so into F1 after that it basically became his whole personality. Carlos was one of the first drivers I came across, and honestly? I wasn't impressed at first. But sometime in early 2024 I saw a photo of him and did a complete 180. Ever since, he's been my favorite driver and F1 has been a huge part of my life — I watch every race, collect merch, spend hours reading about the sport, and have even pulled a few friends into the fandom. I have a giant Carlos flag above my bed. No notes.

Beyond the fandom side of things, F1 genuinely helped me through a rough period in my life. It gave me something to focus on, an escape, a niche that was fully mine. It also turned a friend into my best friend, and we're still close to this day.

As for other drivers — I don't really dislike anyone, except Lance Stroll. If you know, you know.

Lewis Hamilton

Lewis Hamilton, born January 7th, 1985, is arguably the biggest reason F1 has the audience it does today. He's the first and only Black driver in F1 history, and he's never shied away from using his platform — speaking out on LGBTQ+ rights, the genocide in Palestine, and plenty more. He's bigger than the sport, plain and simple.

What I admire most about Lewis and Ocon — born September 17th, 1996 — is where they came from. Unlike most drivers on the grid, neither of them grew up wealthy. That kind of background takes a different level of drive and sacrifice, both from them and their families, and it makes me respect them that much more.

Lando Norris

Lastly, Lando Norris, born November 13th, 1999. There's not a ton I need to say — I just appreciated watching him grind through his early struggles and come out winning. Being younger than most of the grid when he started made him feel a little more relatable to me, which goes a long way.
