Intro to F1

Welcome to the fastest show on earth, Formula 1.

You've probably heard of F1 by now, whether you heard someone talk about it in passing, saw content online, flicked past a Formula 1 race on TV, etc. You may have thought, "It's just cars going in circles." Well, this blog is for you. Because F1 is anything but that.

Formula 1 is the pinnacle of motorsport. With twenty drivers — Carlos Sainz, Alexander Albon, Lewis Hamilton, Charles Leclerc, Kimi Antonelli, George Russell, Gabriel Bortoleto, Nico Hulkenberg, Lance Stroll, Fernando Alonso, Oscar Piastri, Lando Norris, Sergio Perez, Valtteri Bottas, Isack Hadjar, Max Verstappen, Arvid Lindblad, Liam Lawson, Franco Colapinto, Pierre Gasly, Esteban Ocon, and Oliver Bearman — ten teams and twenty-four races spread across five continents, whoever reaches the finish line first wins.

The cars themselves are enough to make a whole blog about — meticulously built machines that can go up to 200 mph, with enough downforce to snap any other athlete's neck in a split second. But the cars are just the beginning.

F1 is made up of more than just the cars: the heated rivalries, the drama, the split-second decisions that can make or break a race, the heartbreak of seeing your favorite driver crash just before the finish line, the charming personalities of every driver. There's something for everyone.

The drivers have more to worry about than just beating each other — cars malfunctioning, weather working against them, losing position over an illegal move. It's a cutthroat sport.

Whether you stumbled here after watching Drive to Survive on Netflix, caught a highlight reel that made your jaw drop, or just want to understand what all the fuss is about — welcome. You're in the right place.
